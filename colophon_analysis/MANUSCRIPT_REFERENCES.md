# Detailed Manuscript References for Colophon Formulas

## For Dissertation Use

This document provides precise manuscript references for all colophon formulas identified in the analysis, including full transcriptions and English translations.

## Formula 1: Finito libro sit laus et gloria Christo

**Category:** Italian General  
**Total instances:** 3  
**Reynhout classification:** Italian General

### Instance 1

**Manuscript:** Florence, Biblioteca Nazionale Centrale di Firenzi, Conv.Soppr.D.VII. 344; Full manuscript  
**Location:** Italy  
**Century:** 15th  
**Institution:** San Jacopo di Ripoli, Florence, Italy  

**TRANSCRIPTION (Original Language):**

> f.203v: "Finito libro isto. Laus et gloria sit domino Iesu Christo. Iesus. Ego soror Angela indigna serva domini nostri Iesu Christi scripsi manu propria hoc collectarium. Deus sit laudatus et pro me deprecatus. Qui scripsit scribat semper cum domino vivat. Vivat in Coelis semper cum domino felix. Facta fine pia benedicat virgo maria."

**TRANSLATION (English):**

> f.203v: "This book is finished. Praise and glory to our Lord Jesus Christ. Jesus. I, Sister Angela, unworthy servant of our Lord Jesus Christ, wrote this breviary with my own hand. May God be praised and prayed for me."

---

### Instance 2

**Manuscript:** Florence, Biblioteca Riccardiana, Ricc.1794; Full manuscript  
**Location:** Italy  
**Century:** 16th  

**TRANSCRIPTION (Original Language):**

> in red or reddish-sepia ink, much faded, f.369 bottom of column of text: “[O]* llaude e gloria dello omnipotente Dio e della gloriosa vergine Maria et di sancto Benedetto, P.N. è finito parte di questo libro, cioè capitoli 40, per mano di suora Paula, indengna serva di Yesu Christo, a consolatione di chi lo leggerà, e a chi piacerà, farà prece a dio con quello affetto di carità col quale essa l’à scripto con licentia della sua superiora.” 
f.370 colophon continues at top of column of text: “Nell’anno della salutifera incarnatione del Nostro Signore Yesu Christo Mccccc90 e a dì X del mese di settenbre. Finis.”

**TRANSLATION (English):**

> "f.369 bottom of column of text: "To the praise and glory of the Almighty God and of the glorious Virgin Mary and of Saint Benedict, P.N., part of this book is finished, namely chapters 40, by the hand of Sister Paula, unworthy servant of Jesus Christ, for the consolation of those who will read it, and to whom it may please, she will pray to God with that affection of charity with which she wrote it, with the permission of her superior."
f.370 colophon continues at top of column of text: "In the year of the salutary incarnation of Our Lord Jesus Christ MCCCCXC, and on the 10th day of the month of September. End."

---

### Instance 3

**Manuscript:** Milan, Biblioteca Nazionale Braidense, AF.IX.68; Full manuscript  
**Location:** Italy  
**Century:** 15th  

**TRANSCRIPTION (Original Language):**

> f.112r: Finito libro laus sit gloria Christo. Hunc Comitisa virens annis teneraque inventa / transcripsit librum Matre favente Dei. Vivida quam virtus radiat celerisque propago / exornatque decor atque pudicitia.

**TRANSLATION (English):**

> “With the book finished, let praise and glory be to Christ.
This book was transcribed by a young countess, blooming in years and tender of age, with the favor of the Mother of God.
Lively virtue shines in her, swift descent adorns her, as do grace and modesty.”

## Formula 2: Qui scripsit scribat semper cum Domino uiuat

**Category:** Italian General  
**Total instances:** 10  
**Reynhout classification:** Italian General

### Instance 1

**Manuscript:** Bergamo, Biblioteca Civica Angelo Mai, MA 113; Full manuscript  
**Location:** Italy  
**Century:** 15th  

**TRANSCRIPTION (Original Language):**

> f.95r: “Deo gratias. Amen. XVIII marcii. Deo gratias. Qui scripsit scribat, semper cum Domino vivat. Vivat in celis semper cum Domino felix. Ego Comina (Comina is erased and substituted with ‘domina Iacoma’ in a different but contemporary hand) scripsi istum libellum cum sua (word erased and substituted with 'mea' in same hand as made the correction above) propria manu. MCCCCLII. E ve pregi per carità che voliati pregare Dio per mi. Finito libro referamus gratia Christo.”

**TRANSLATION (English):**

> f.95r: "Thanks be to God. Amen. 18th of March. Thanks be to God. May the one who wrote it always write, and always live with the Lord. May they live happily in heaven forever with the Lord. I, Comina (Comina is erased and substituted with ‘Lady Iacoma’ in a different but contemporary hand), wrote this little book with my own (word erased and substituted with 'my' in the same hand as made the correction above) hand. 1452. And I ask you out of charity to please pray to God for me. Having finished the book, let us give thanks to Christ."

"From the library of the Monastery of the Body of Christ, in the hand of Illuminata Bembo, certified by the abbess Laura Beccadelli."

## Formula 3: Finis adest operis merce dem poseo laboris

**Category:** Germanic  
**Total instances:** 1  
**Reynhout classification:** Germanic

### Instance 1

**Manuscript:** Munich, Bayerische Staatsbibliothek, Clm 23046; Full manuscript  
**Century:** 14th  

**TRANSCRIPTION (Original Language):**

> (Imago Adelhaidis quae codicem scripsit); Hernad 2000 p. 45 transcribes the text "Finis adest operis mercedem posco laboris. Et qui me scribebat Adelhaidis nomen habebat. Sanctus Bernhardus. Dominus noster Jhesus Christus ipse erit merces tua, fiat fiat." (144r); Adelhaid is before Hl. Bernhard.

**TRANSLATION (English):**

> “The end of the work is here, I ask for the reward of my labor. And the one who wrote me had the name Adelhaid. Saint Bernard. Our Lord Jesus Christ himself will be your reward, let it be, let it be.”
