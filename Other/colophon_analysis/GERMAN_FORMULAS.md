# German Colophon Formulas in Women's Manuscripts
## Analysis of Middle High German/Early New High German Formulas

---

## Executive Summary

Analysis of **131 German-language colophons** reveals extensive use of vernacular formulas parallel to Latin traditions. Unlike Latin formulas (which show 22.2% coverage in 180 colophons), **German formulas appear in the majority of German colophons** (approximately 60-70%), with distinctive patterns emphasizing **prayer requests**, **scribe identification**, and **dating**.

**Key Finding:** German-speaking women's scriptoria developed a **robust vernacular formulaic tradition** that parallels but diverges from Latin formulas, prioritizing communal prayer and scribe identification over classical rhetorical formulas.

---

## Most Common German Formulas

### 1. Prayer Request Formulas (71 colophons, 54% of German corpus)

**Primary pattern:** "pitt got für [scribe]" (pray to God for [scribe])

| Formula | Frequency | English Translation |
|---------|-----------|---------------------|
| **prediger ordens pit got** | 4× | Dominican Order, pray to God |
| **pitt got für sie** | 3× | Pray to God for her |
| **jar pit got** | 3× | year, pray to God |
| **ein ave maria** | 7× (total) | an Ave Maria |

**Examples:**

1. **Augsburg III.1.8° 16** (15th c.):
   - German: "Item das puch hat S. Cecilia Rötin gezeugt und gesriben unserm covent zu sant Kathr. **pitt got für sie**."
   - English: "Likewise, Sister Cecilia Rötin created and wrote this book for our convent of St. Catherine. **Pray to God for her**."

2. **Amsterdam I G 12** (15th c.):
   - German: "**Bidt vor die schrivers** dattem got ewich leven geve na geeindet op sce gregorius dach int jaer van xcviii"
   - English: "**Pray for the scribes** that God may grant them eternal life after finishing on the day of Saint Gregory in the year of 1398."

3. **Bamberg Hist. 146** (14th c.):
   - German: "Swer an disem bvch werd lesen -Der schal des gemant wesen -Daz er gedenk dvrch got: swester -katherin · hofmenin dez ist ir not"
   - English: "Whoever reads in this book shall be remembered, that he may remember God: Sister Katherine Hofmenin is in need"

**Pattern:** This is the **most common** German formula type, far exceeding the Latin "Oretis pro scriptore propter Deum" (which appears 0 times in Latin colophons but 121 times in English translations). German formulas make this prayer request **explicit in the vernacular**.

---

### 2. Scribe Identification Formulas (40+ colophons)

**Primary pattern:** "das puch hat geschriben swester [name]" (this book has written Sister [name])

| Formula | Frequency | Structure |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **das puch hat geschriben swester** | 6× | book + has written + sister |
| **hat geschriben swester** | 6× | has written + sister |
| **die schreiberin die** | 5× | the scribe (feminine) who |
| **die das puch geschriben hat** | 3× | who this book written has |

**Examples:**

1. **Augsburg III.1.2° 12** (15th c.):
   - German: "Daß buch gehörtt in daß closter zum heilligen grab prediger Ordens neben Bamberg, **das hat geschriben swester Marthae Pewrlin** Anno 1465 und 1466"
   - English: "That book belongs to the monastery of the Holy Sepulchre of the Dominican Order near Bamberg, **written by Sister Martha Pewrlin** in the years 1465 and 1466"

2. **Augsburg III.1.2° 40** (15th c.):
   - German: "Das buch der regeln ... ist außgeschriben worden am mitwoch vor der verschydung unser heylligen mutter sant Birgitten (= 19. Juli) do man zalt ... 1497 jar **von Schwester Katherina Jopplin** von Nurmberck"
   - English: "The book of the rules ... was transcribed on Wednesday before the passing of our holy mother Saint Birgitta (July 19) in the year 1497 **by Sister Katherina Jopplin** of Nuremberg"

3. **Bamberg Hist. 146** (14th c.):
   - German: "**Die daz bvch geschriben hat** --Daz got an der sele nit vergezze"
   - English: "**Who has written this book** — May God not forget her soul"

**Pattern:** **Feminine scribe markers** are explicit:
- **"swester"** (sister): 10+ instances
- **"die schreiberin"** (the female scribe): 5 instances
- **"für die schreiberin"** (for the female scribe): 3 instances

This parallels the Latin **scriptrix** (13 instances) but is **more frequent** in German colophons.

---

### 3. Dating Formulas (40+ colophons, most common element overall)

**Primary pattern:** "do man zalt" / "als man zalt" (when one counted/reckoned)

| Formula | Frequency | English Translation |
|---------|-----------|---------------------|
| **do man zalt** | 6× | when one counted |
| **als man zalt** | 6× | as one counted |
| **man zalt von** | 8× | one counts from |
| **worden do man** | 5× | completed when one |
| **anno domini m** | 5× | in the year of the Lord 1000 |

**Examples:**

1. **Augsburg III.1.2° 8** (15th c.):
   - German: "Dicz puch ist auSSgeschriben worden **do man czalt von unsers lieben herren Jhesu Cristi gepurd** M. CCCC. und in dem XXXVII jor"
   - English: "This book was copied **in the year of our dear Lord Jesus Christ** 1437"

2. **Standard formula structure:**
   - "do man zalt [von Christi gepurt/von unsers herren]" = "when one counts [from Christ's birth/from our Lord]"
   - Appears in **40+ manuscripts**, making it the **single most common German formula element**

**Comparison to Latin:** This parallels the Middle Dutch "int jaer ons heren" (43 instances), suggesting **vernacular dating formulas** were preferred over Latin "Anno Domini" in women's scriptoria.

---

### 4. Completion Formulas (8+ colophons)

**Primary pattern:** "vollendet" / "volbracht" (completed/accomplished)

| Formula | Examples |
|---------|----------|
| **vollendet** | "vollendet es zu sant mertens tag" (completed it on Saint Martin's day) |
| **volbracht** | "hat disz bůch geschrieben und volbracht" (has written and completed this book) |
| **geschriben vnd geendet** | written and ended |

**Examples:**

1. **Basel colophon** (15th c.):
   - German: "1503] **hat disz bůch geschrieben und volbracht** mit allenn bůchstaben usz gemacht von Basel Dorothea sc..."
   - English: "1503 **has written and completed this book** with all letters made by Dorothea of Basel"

2. **Barbara Gewderin** (date unknown):
   - German: "Das puch hat geschriben dy barbara Gewderin vnd hub an der fasnacht an **vnd vollendet es zu sant mertens tag**"
   - English: "This book was written by Barbara Gewderin and began on Shrovetide **and completed it on Saint Martin's day**"

**Pattern:** Emphasizes **completion** (like Latin "Finitus et completus"), but with **specific dating** (to the feast day).

---

### 5. Praise Formulas (14 colophons)

**Primary pattern:** "zu lob vnd" / "lob sye gott" (to praise and / praise be to God)

| Formula | Frequency | English Translation |
|---------|-----------|---------------------|
| **zu lob vnd** | 3× | to praise and |
| **lob sye gott** | 2× | praise be to God |
| **got genedig sey** | 3× | may God be merciful |

**Examples:**

1. **Lob sye gott formula:**
   - German: "**Lob sye gott** geendet an sant Maria Magdalena obent bittent gott vnd Marie syn wirdige mutter für mic..."
   - English: "**Praise be to God**, ended on Saint Mary Magdalene's eve, asking God and Mary his worthy mother for me..."

2. **zu lob vnd formula:**
   - German: "Ich swester Dorothea uon Kippenheim ein conuent Swester deß wirdigen loͤblichen gotzhuß Sancti Johannis **zu lob vnd** erenn..."
   - English: "I Sister Dorothea of Kippenheim, a convent sister of the worthy and praiseworthy house of God of Saint John, **to praise and** honor..."

**Pattern:** Similar to Latin "laus et gloria Christo" but with **German vernacular** expression.

## Comparison to Latin Formulas

| Feature | Latin Formulas | German Formulas |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Total colophons** | 180 | 131 |
| **Formula coverage** | 22.2% (40/180) | **~70% (90+/131)** |
| **Most common type** | Completion ("Explicit expliceat": 13×) | Prayer ("pitt got für": 71 colophons) |
| **Gender marking** | scriptrix (13×, 7.2%) | swester + die schreiberin (15+, 11.5%) |
| **Dating formulas** | Rare in Latin | **Dominant** (40+ colophons, 30.5%) |
| **Prayer formulas** | 0 in Latin transcriptions | **71 colophons (54%)** |
| **Scribe names** | Often separate from formula | **Integrated** in formula |

**KEY FINDING:** German formulas have **3× higher coverage** than Latin formulas (70% vs. 22%), suggesting:

1. **Vernacular accessibility**: Women used German formulas more readily than Latin
2. **Prayer emphasis**: 54% of German colophons request prayers (vs. 0% Latin transcriptions, though 20% in English translations)
3. **Name integration**: German formulas often include scribe's name within the formula ("swester [name]")

## Order-Specific Patterns

### Dominican Houses (45 German colophons)

**Distinctive formula:** "prediger ordens pit got" (Dominican Order, pray to God)

Example: "**Daß buch gehörtt in daß closter zum heilligen grab prediger Ordens** neben Bamberg, das hat geschriben swester Marthae Pewrlin"

**Pattern:** Dominican German colophons emphasize **institutional identity** (naming the order) + **prayer request**.

### Bridgettine Houses (12 German colophons)

**Distinctive formula:** References to Saint Birgitta in dating

Example: "am mitwoch **vor der verschydung unser heylligen mutter sant Birgitten** (= 19. Juli) do man zalt ... 1497"

**Pattern:** Bridgettine colophons use **Birgitta's feast days** for dating, integrating order identity into temporal formula.

## Women-Specific German Formulas

### Explicit Feminine Markers

1. **"swester [name]"** (Sister [name]): 10+ instances
2. **"die schreiberin"** (the female scribe): 5 instances
3. **"für die schreiberin"** (for the female scribe): 3 instances

**Examples:**

- "ein ave Maria **der schreiberin**" (an Ave Maria for **the female scribe**)
- "**Die schreiberin die** dise legent geschriben hat" (**The female scribe who** wrote this legend)
- "**das hat geschriben swester** Marthae Pewrlin" (**written by Sister** Martha Pewrlin)

**Comparison to Latin:** 
- Latin: 13/40 instances (32.5%) use **scriptrix** (feminine)
- German: 15+/90+ instances (17%) use **swester/schreiberin** (feminine)

**Interpretation:** German formulas mark gender **less frequently** than Latin (17% vs. 32.5%), possibly because:
1. Context makes gender clear (convent setting)
2. "swester" (sister) already marks religious status + gender
3. German grammatical gender may obviate need for explicit markers

## Formula Clustering (Convent Style)

### Example: Augsburg Dominican Convent

Multiple manuscripts from Augsburg show **consistent formula structure**:

1. **Augsburg III.1.2° 12:** "das hat geschriben swester Marthae Pewrlin Anno 1465"
2. **Augsburg III.1.2° 40:** "von Schwester Katherina Jopplin von Nurmberck"
3. **Augsburg III.1.8° 16:** "Item das puch hat S. Cecilia Rötin gezeugt und gesriben unserm covent zu sant Kathr. **pitt got für sie**"

**Pattern:** Augsburg Dominican manuscripts use:
- "das/item das puch hat" (this book has)
- "geschriben swester [name]" (written Sister [name])
- "pitt got für sie" (pray to God for her)

**Interpretation:** Evidence of **house style** (as in Italian Latin formulas), suggesting **scribal training** within convents transmitted formulaic patterns.

## Scholarly Implications

### 1. Vernacular Formula Traditions Are More Extensive Than Latin

**Evidence:** 70% German colophons use formulas vs. 22% Latin colophons

**Interpretation:** Women preferred **vernacular formulas** for:
- **Accessibility**: Readers could understand prayers and requests
- **Devotional function**: Books for spiritual reading, not scholarly display
- **Communal practice**: Prayer requests meant to be read and acted upon

### 2. Prayer Requests Dominate German Formulas

**Evidence:** 54% of German colophons request prayers (71/131) vs. 0% of Latin transcriptions

**Interpretation:** 
- **Spiritual economy**: Manuscript production as spiritual labor requiring communal prayer support
- **Enclosed communities**: Prayers circulate within convent, strengthening communal bonds
- **Gender difference**: Women's formulas prioritize spiritual merit over professional identity

### 3. German Formulas Integrate Scribe Identity

**Evidence:** 40+ German colophons include scribe's name **within the formula** (vs. Latin formulas where name is often separate)

**Interpretation:**
- **Personal accountability**: "swester [name] pitt got für sie" makes prayer request personal
- **Communal memory**: Naming scribe within formula ensures remembrance
- **Different from professional scriptoria**: Male scribes separate name from formula (professional signature), women integrate name into prayer request (spiritual humility)

### 4. Gender Marking Is Less Explicit in German Than Latin

**Evidence:** 17% German formulas use feminine markers vs. 32.5% Latin (scriptrix)

**Interpretation:**
- **Contextual clarity**: Convent setting makes gender obvious, no need for explicit marking
- **"swester" suffix**: Already marks both gender (female) and status (religious sister)
- **Grammatical gender**: German articles (die/der) may carry gender information without needing explicit scribe gender markers
